Featured Project

Warren Buffett Knowledge Assistant

This project is a powerful Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system built with Streamlit and powered by the Gemini AI model. It allows users to ask complex questions and receive accurate, verifiable answers based on a knowledge base of Berkshire Hathaway annual reports (2008-2025).

Key Features:

  • Accurate, context-aware answers from provided documents.
  • Source citations to eliminate "hallucination."
  • Intuitive and clean user interface built with Streamlit.
  • Scalable architecture for various document types.

Demonstration: Questions & Answers

The following examples showcase the RAG system's ability to go beyond simple keyword searches. It can provide a truly insightful, synthesized, and well-supported answer by referencing multiple documents, which is exactly what a high-quality RAG system should do.

Question:

"What does Warren Buffett's correspondence in the annual reports say about the role of insurance float in Berkshire's long-term success?"

Answer:

Based on the annual reports from 2004 to 2024, Warren Buffett emphasizes the importance of insurance float as a 'cost-free' source of capital. This float allows Berkshire to make opportunistic investments, particularly during economic downturns, without the need for traditional debt. He highlights how the insurance operations, even in loss years, provide this valuable capital for investment.

Question:

"Can you explain the impact of inflation on businesses, according to Buffett, and what he recommends to protect against it?"

Answer:

Warren Buffett describes inflation as a 'pesticide' and 'destructive force' that erodes the purchasing power of money and makes it difficult for businesses with low pricing power to maintain profits. He recommends investing in businesses that possess strong "economic moats" and can raise prices in line with inflation, such as those with unique brands or low-cost production advantages, to protect against its negative effects.

Question:

"Based on his letters, what qualities does Warren Buffett look for in a candidate for a director of the board?"

Answer:

The provided context from the Berkshire Hathaway Proxy Statements details the attributes Buffett and his team look for in candidates for directors of the board. These include very high integrity, being business savvy, an owner-oriented attitude, and a deep, genuine interest in Berkshire. The documents highlight that a significant investment in Berkshire shares is also seen as a positive indicator, aligning the director's interests with those of the shareholders.
